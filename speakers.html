Title of the Talk: On the Need For Simple But Powerful Optimization Techniques

×
Abstract : One of the strengths of metaheuristics such as evolutionary algorithms and tabu search is that they are general ideas that can rather easily be applied to a new optimization problem at hand. In many cases, however, a straightforward implementation of a metaheuristic to an optimization problem does not reach state-of-the-art performance. Therefore, researchers often equip their metaheuristic with problem-specific bits and pieces that might be rather complicated to code. On the other side, the application and implementation of high-performance Operations Research (OR) techniques for optimization are often not achievable for non-experts. In other words, especially in industry, there is a need for high-performance optimization techniques that can be easily implemented by non-experts. In this talk we are going to explain that "Construct, Merge, Solve & Adapt (CMSA)", a new but simple algorithm combining heuristic elements with OR expertise, can fill this gap.

Title of the Talk: Framework for Multi Cloud Collaboration

×
Abstract : There is a need to develop mechanisms that allow opportunistic collaboration among services without requiring tight standards and also should not do extensive changes to the cloud service delivery model. From a market perspective, it is unlikely that multiple CSPs will agree on an easy and standardized way to access services in collaboration, as this would give clients total freedom to switch to other providers services, it also brings open and direct competition with other providers. CSPs often offer differentiated services with specialized proprietary products and services. We can overcome these restrictions by using network of proxies. A proxy is an edge-node-hosted software instance that a client or a CSP can delegate to carry out operations on its behalf. To facilitate dynamic collaboration between clouds, I would like to discuss a framework that uses proxies to act as mediators between multiple clouds that can share data.

Title of the Talk: On Data Interoperability, Reliability, and Image Processing in IoT

×
Abstract : It discusses challenges associated with the Internet of Things(IoT), Edge Computing, data interoperability, and Reliability. It sails on foundation concepts with a direct impact on the data collection process. The perspectives of Data ownership, data sovereignty, and data confidentiality are analyzed in the light of the decision-making process and real-time applications. Thus, the importance of commonalities and building block reuse is addressed. In this context, a few reference implementations and the OpenVino framework are quickly presented for exploding the potentiality of image processing. In this scenario, AI ethics is discussed to put in context its importance for data processing.
